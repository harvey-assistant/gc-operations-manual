## ðŸ“‹ FORMAT 1: ITERATIVE DESIGN (Jordan Model)

### Overview
- **Who runs:** Jordan typically

### Session 1: Outcomes Clarification (10% Clear)

**Goal:** Get crystal clear on transformation you're selling

**Prep:**
- [ ] Review kickoff notes
- [ ] Review intake form outcomes
- [ ] Prepare clarifying questions

**Opening:**
```

```

**Key questions:**
```

â–¡ "What transformation do they want most?"
â–¡ "What are they trying to avoid?"
â–¡ "What's the gap between where they are and where they want to be?"
â–¡ "What are they currently doing that doesn't work?"
```

**Listen for:**
- [ ] Verbatim customer language
- [ ] What they're ESCAPING (enemy positioning)
- [ ] Quantifiable gaps (numbers, metrics)
- [ ] Conflicts between outcomes

**Output:**
- [ ] Top 3 outcomes prioritized
- [ ] Transformation statement drafted
- [ ] Enemy positioning identified

**Real example (Austin Cohen):**

> Refined: "Help 250-300K/year chiropractors hit 50K/month WITHOUT diluting

### Session 3: Offer Structure + Pricing (99% Clear)

**Goal:** Define what you're selling and how much

**Prep:**
- [ ] Review sessions 1-2 notes
- [ ] Draft 2-3 offer structure options
- [ ] Research comparable pricing

**Opening:**
```

   â””â”€ How much time can they realistically invest?

   â””â”€ What are they escaping?
```

**Pricing methodology:**

**Step 1: Outcome-based pricing**
```

If membership helps them get there, what % of that value is fair?
â†’ Even 1% = $6,000/year = $500/month

**Step 2: Stair-step approach (Alex Hormozi model)**
```
START LOW:
- First 10-50 people: $47-75/month (founding member pricing)
- Validate demand, get testimonials, iron out kinks

INCREASE GRADUALLY:

- Next 50: Increase 10% â†’ $57-91/month

- Early adopters take risk â†’ rewarded with lower price

```

**Step 3: Personal budget threshold**
```
SWEET SPOT: <$100/month
- Doesn't require spouse approval
- Can expense or pay personally
- "Impulse buy" territory for professionals

DANGER ZONE: >$150/month
- Requires justification
- Higher churn risk
- Harder sell
```

**Output:**
- [ ] Offer format decided
- [ ] Deliverables defined
- [ ] Pricing validated
- [ ] Positioning locked

**Real example (Luca Feb 4):**
> **Offer:** Membership (not cohort)
> **Deliverables:** Weekly content drops, community, quarterly live events
> **Pricing:** $47-75/month start, stair-step to $100
> **Positioning:** "Make your team ship faster" (not "grow your career")

### Session 4: Validation + Finalization

**Goal:** Test with real customers, lock in final decisions

**Prep:**
- [ ] Draft messaging for testing
- [ ] Identify 5-10 people to validate with

**Process:**
```
1. Reach out to 5-10 ideal customers
2. Pitch the transformation (not features)
3. Ask: "Would you pay $X/month for this?"
4. Listen for objections
5. Refine based on feedback
```

**Validation script:**
```
"Hey [Name], quick question:

I'm working on [transformation statement] for [target avatar]. 

The format is [offer structure], and it's $X/month.

Honest feedback: Would this be valuable to you? Any hesitations?"
```

**What to listen for:**
- [ ] Price objections (too high? too low?)
- [ ] Format objections (wrong delivery method?)
- [ ] Transformation objections (not the right outcome?)
- [ ] Timing objections (not ready? urgency missing?)

**Output:**
- [ ] Final pricing confirmed
- [ ] Messaging refined based on objections
- [ ] 2-5 early commitments (ideally)
- [ ] Green light to BUILD phase

## ðŸ“‹ FORMAT 2: STRUCTURED WORKSHOP (Filip Model)

### Overview
- **Who runs:** Filip (or Jordan)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes intensive
- **Best for:** Experienced clients who respond to frameworks
- **Example:** Luca Rossi Feb 4 workshop

### Workshop Structure

**Prep (30 min before):**
- [ ] Review kickoff notes
- [ ] Prepare empathy mapping canvas
- [ ] Have framework slides ready

**Section 1: Empathy Mapping (30 min)**

**Framework: SAY â†’ THINK â†’ FEEL â†’ DO** (Jake Wysocki method)

```
QUADRANT 1: WHAT DO THEY SAY?
"What words do your ideal customers use to describe their problem?"
[Capture exact phrases, verbatim language]

QUADRANT 2: WHAT DO THEY THINK?
"What's going through their head? Internal dialogue?"
[Deeper beliefs, assumptions, fears]

QUADRANT 3: WHAT DO THEY FEEL?
"What emotions are they experiencing?"
[Frustration, anxiety, hope, desperation]

QUADRANT 4: WHAT DO THEY DO?
"What actions are they taking (or not taking)?"
[Behaviors, workarounds, failed solutions]
```

**Output:**
- [ ] Empathy map completed
- [ ] Customer language captured
- [ ] Problem deeply understood

---

**Section 2: Promise Statement Refinement (30 min)**

**Jake Wysocki's "Brain on Speakerphone" method:**

```
STEP 1: Write initial promise
"We help [WHO] achieve [WHAT]"

STEP 2: Make thinking visible
- What assumptions am I making?
- What am I taking for granted?
- What needs to be more specific?

STEP 3: Refine based on questions
- Is WHO specific enough?
- Is WHAT quantifiable?
- Is this believable?
- Does it differentiate from alternatives?

STEP 4: Test for clarity
"If I told this to an ideal customer, would they immediately say 
'That's exactly what I need'?"
```

**Real example (Luca workshop):**
> Initial: "Help engineering managers be better leaders"
> Refined: "Make your team ship faster" (outcome-focused, quantifiable)

**Output:**
- [ ] Promise statement refined
- [ ] Customer reaction tested
- [ ] Differentiation clear

---

**Section 3: Offer Design + Pricing (30 min)**

**Use WHO â†’ PROBLEMS â†’ PROMISE â†’ DELIVERABLES â†’ PRICING sequence:**

```

```markdown

**Drafting time:** 2-4 hours

---

**Step 2: Client Review (Async)**

Send brief with this framing:
```

```

**Client marks up:**
- [ ] What resonates
- [ ] What's off
- [ ] What's missing
- [ ] Questions/concerns

**Step 3: Review Call (60 min)**

**Agenda:**
```
1. What resonated (5 min)
2. What felt off (15 min)
3. What's missing (10 min)
4. Refine together (20 min)
5. Lock in decisions (10 min)
```

**Output:**
- [ ] Final strategy brief
- [ ] All decisions locked
- [ ] Green light to BUILD phase

### 2. "Brain on Speakerphone"

**Purpose:** Make invisible thinking visible, surface assumptions

**Method:**
```

**Test:**
- [ ] Would ideal customer immediately say "That's me!"?
- [ ] Is it differentiated from alternatives?

## âš ï¸ COMMON MISTAKES

**Mistake 1: Trying to nail everything in one session**
- Design takes 3-4 weeks for a reason
- Solution: Use 10% â†’ 50% â†’ 99% model

**Mistake 2: Skipping customer validation**
- Designing in isolation without real feedback
- Solution: Always validate with 5-10 real customers

**Mistake 3: Generic positioning**
- "Help entrepreneurs grow their business"
- Solution: Use verbatim customer language, quantify gaps

**Mistake 4: Pricing based on costs (not value)**
- "I need to charge $X to cover my costs"
- Solution: Price on outcome value, not your expenses

**Mistake 5: Wrong format for client**
- Using workshop for someone who needs hand-holding
- Solution: Match format to client's style
