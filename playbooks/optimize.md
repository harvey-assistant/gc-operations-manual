### Exit Survey Setup

**Two cancellation paths:**

**Path 1: Managed (WITH survey):**
```

  â†“
Tally form: "What would you like to do?"
  â”œâ”€ Update payment â†’ Redirect to Circle billing
  â””â”€ Cancel subscription â†’ Exit survey
```

**Path 2: Direct (BYPASS survey):**
```

  â†“
Skips exit survey
```

**Strategy:**
- Enable self-cancellation (don't force permission)
- Make managed path prominent in FAQs
- Accept not everyone will fill survey (maybe 50-70%)
- Capture what you can

**Jake's wisdom:**
> "We're okay with some percentage not filling it out. We make the FAQs 
> prominent with the link, and we get what we can. Can't force 100%."

---

### Exit Survey Questions

**Question 1: Why are you leaving?** (Multiple choice)
- [ ] Too expensive
- [ ] Not enough value
- [ ] Not enough time
- [ ] Found another solution
- [ ] Other: [text field]

**Question 2: What would have made you stay?** (Free text)

**Question 3: How likely to recommend?** (NPS 0-10 scale)

**Question 4: Can we follow up?** (Email for potential win-back)

---

### Analyzing Churn

**Monthly churn review:**
```
1. Pull exit survey responses
2. Group by reason (category breakdown)
3. Identify patterns:
   - One reason dominating? (fix that)
   - Multiple scattered reasons? (broader issue)
   - No clear pattern? (natural churn, acceptable)

4. Prioritize fixes:
   - HIGH: Affects >30% of churners
   - MEDIUM: Affects 10-30%
   - LOW: One-off issues
```

**Don't try to save everyone.** Some churn is natural.

## ğŸ”‘ KEY FRAMEWORKS

### 1. Short Weekly Syncs

**Concept:** 30-45 min, focused on action (not status updates)

**Why it works:**
- Keeps team aligned
- Surfaces issues fast
- Forces prioritization
- Creates momentum

**Anti-pattern:** 90-min meandering status meetings

---

### 2. Manual Workarounds While Investigating

**Concept:** Don't wait for perfect fix, manually handle while debugging

**Filip's approach:**
> "I cannot identify the pattern, so I've reached out to Circle. Until then, 
> I'll manually check daily and kick out canceled members."

**Why it works:**
- Problem gets handled NOW
- Doesn't block progress
- Root cause investigation happens in parallel

---

### 3. One Big Thing Per Week

**Concept:** Limit improvements to 1 major thing per week

**Why it works:**
- Can measure what moved the needle
- Team stays focused
- Compound improvements over time

**Anti-pattern:** 10 initiatives, nothing ships
