## ðŸšª CHURN MANAGEMENT

### Pattern: Exit Survey â†’ Analysis â†’ Win-Back (Sometimes)

**The cancellation flow challenge:**

**Two paths to cancel:**
1. **Managed cancellation:** Member clicks "Manage Account" â†’ Tally form â†’ Exit survey â†’ Cancel
2. **Direct cancellation:** Member goes to billing â†’ Cancel (bypasses survey)

**Jake's dilemma:**
> "If we're okay with some percentage of people not filling out the exit survey, and maybe we make the FAQs more prominent, like if you have to update your account, go to this link, then it probably solves it. Just knowing that you're not going to get 100% of the people to fill it out."

**Solution: Balance convenience vs data**
- Enable self-cancellation (don't make people ask permission)
- Prominently link to managed flow in FAQs
- Accept that not everyone will fill out survey
- Capture what you can, don't make friction too high

---

### Exit Survey Best Practices

**What to ask:**
1. **Why are you leaving?** (Multiple choice + other)
   - Too expensive
   - Not enough value
   - Not enough time
   - Found another solution
   - Other (explain)

2. **What would have made you stay?**
   - Free text response

3. **How likely are you to recommend?** (NPS)
   - 0-10 scale

4. **Can we follow up?**
   - Email for potential win-back

**What to track:**
- Churn reasons (aggregate over time)
- NPS trends
- Win-back success rate
- Lifetime value by cohort

## ðŸš¨ COMMON OPTIMIZE MISTAKES

**Mistake 1: Not tracking churn reasons**
- Flying blind on why people leave
- Can't fix what you don't measure
- Solution: Exit survey, analyze quarterly

**Mistake 2: Building before validating**
- Spend weeks on features nobody wants
- Opportunity cost of not building right thing
- Solution: Ask â†’ Test â†’ Build

**Mistake 3: Ignoring support volume**
- Same questions over and over
- Team overwhelmed
- Solution: FAQ, automation, documentation

**Mistake 4: Not firing bad facilitators**
- One bad experience kills retention
- "We can't find anyone better" paralysis
- Solution: Hire, train, evaluate, replace

**Mistake 5: Optimizing too many things at once**
- Can't tell what worked
- Team confused, scattered effort
- Solution: One big thing per week
